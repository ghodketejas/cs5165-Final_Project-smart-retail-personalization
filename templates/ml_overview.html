ML models (course write-up)

Under 200 words · Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) focus

Linear Regression models a numeric target as a weighted sum of features. It trains quickly, stays easy to explain to stakeholders, and works well when relationships are roughly smooth and you want coefficients that read like business levers.

Random Forest averages predictions from many decision trees trained on bootstrap samples and random feature subsets. It captures nonlinear patterns and interactions with less hand tuning than a single tree, though full interpretability often relies on importance scores or partial dependence plots.

Gradient Boosting adds trees sequentially so each new tree reduces the residual error of the ensemble. It frequently delivers strong accuracy on tabular retail data but needs more care with depth, learning rate, and stopping to avoid overfitting on smaller samples.

CLV choice for this project: We use Linear Regression for Customer Lifetime Value because CLV is introduced as a smooth function of interpretable household metrics (spend, baskets, units, active weeks), which supports transparent prioritization of high-value customers. Random Forest or Gradient Boosting would be natural upgrades if we needed sharper segment boundaries or richer interaction effects at the cost of more opaque models.
