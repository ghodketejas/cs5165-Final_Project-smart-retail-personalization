ML model write-up

Retail transaction data powers CLV estimation, basket intelligence, and churn-style recency signals.

1. Customer Lifetime Value Prediction

CLV is predicted using a Linear Regression model trained on household spending behavior, including total spend, total units, total baskets, active weeks, and average basket value. Higher predicted CLV indicates households that may generate more long-term revenue.

Model Details

Model Used: Linear Regression

Features: Total Spend, Total Units, Total Baskets, Active Weeks, Average Basket Value

Target: Estimated Future Customer Lifetime Value

2. Basket Analysis / Cross-Selling

Basket analysis identifies product categories that are commonly purchased together. These combinations can support cross-selling, bundle design, and personalized recommendations.

Model / technique details

Association view: Basket pair frequency (which commodities appear together).

ML view (deliverable): Gradient Boosting classifier on basket-level features to explain which structural signals align with above-median basket spend (cross-sell / upsell opportunity baskets).

Retail use: Pair lists drive bundles; GB importances highlight basket “shape” worth targeting.

3. Churn Prediction

Churn risk is estimated using customer purchase recency. A household is marked as high risk if it has not made a purchase in the last 8 or more weeks of the dataset.

Churn Logic

Metric: Weeks Since Last Purchase

High Risk Rule: 8 or more weeks without purchase activity

Retail Use: Identify households that may need retention offers.
